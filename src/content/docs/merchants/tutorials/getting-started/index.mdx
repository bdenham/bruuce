Your journey to learn how to create new product pages, campaigns, and experiments within Adobe Commerce Storefront begins here. In this topic, you will learn how to use the Storefront Builder to make changes and additions to your company's Adobe Commerce Storefront powered by Edge Delivery Services. As a marketing professional, you will optimize the storefront for conversions and create new product pages, marketing campaigns, and customer conversion experiments.

## Tutorial Scenario

Apple is releasing its latest iPhone. Your company, CitiSignal, wants to launch a catalog update and promotional campaign related to this event. The goal is to increase sales and customer conversions. 

## Tutorial Goals

You will learn how to:

**Lesson 1:** Create a unique product details page <br/>
**Lesson 2:** Run a promotional campaign targeted at general and loyal customers<br/>
**Lesson 3:** Experiment with customer conversions in the cart.

## Tutorial Warmup

Before you get started with the lessons, make sure you can successfully execute the following steps on your workstation:

<Steps>
1. Open any document in your project by selecting it with a **single click for editing** and previewing.
1. Use the **Instant Preview** menu on the right to see all the changes you make to the page in real-time.
1. Revert changes by using the **Undo** button on the Edit menu or using Cmd+Z on your keyboard.
1. Access images in the **AEM Assets library** from the Edit menu: **Library** > **AEM Assets**.
1. Preview any document in your CitiSignal site by selecting the **Action** button (paper-plane icon, top-right), then selecting the **Preview** button.
</Steps>

## Tutorial Tips 

<Steps>
1. **Follow the steps** in order and check the screenshots.  
1. Use the **Instant Preview** menu often.
1. Select the **Action** (paper-plane icon) > **Preview** button on occasion to view your changes.
1. Select the **Library** > **AEM Assets** menu to find images.
1. **Copy and paste** blocks to save time.
1. **Undo** changes if you make a mistake.
</Steps>
